Common Plumbing Problems in New Hampshire Homes

Frozen and Burst Pipes

New Hampshire winters regularly push temperatures well below zero, and frozen pipes are one of the most common — and costly — plumbing emergencies we respond to. When water freezes inside a pipe, it expands and can split copper, PVC, or galvanized steel. If you suspect a frozen pipe (reduced or no water flow, frost on visible pipes, or an unusual gurgling sound), call us immediately before the pipe bursts and causes extensive water damage.

Leaky Faucets and Fixtures

A dripping faucet wastes thousands of gallons per year and can significantly inflate your water bill. Most faucet leaks are caused by worn cartridges, O-rings, or valve seats — components we stock on every service vehicle so repairs are completed the same day. We service all major faucet brands including Moen, Delta, Kohler, American Standard, and Pfister.

Running Toilets

A constantly running toilet can waste up to 200 gallons of water per day — costing NH homeowners $100 or more per month in unnecessary water charges. The culprit is usually a worn flapper, faulty fill valve, or deteriorated flush valve seat. We rebuild or replace toilet internals quickly and affordably, and can also recommend water-efficient WaterSense models if an upgrade makes sense.

Water Pressure Problems

Low water pressure throughout your NH home often points to a failing pressure reducing valve (PRV), mineral buildup in pipes (common with NH's hard water), or a municipal supply issue. Inconsistent pressure — strong one moment, weak the next — may indicate a partially closed main valve or a developing pipe leak. We diagnose pressure issues with calibrated gauges and address the root cause, not just the symptom.

Galvanized and Aging Pipes

Many older New Hampshire homes — particularly those built before 1970 — still have original galvanized steel pipes. As these corrode from the inside out, you'll notice rust-colored water, frequent leaks at joints, and steadily declining pressure. Our plumbers assess the condition of your existing pipes and provide honest recommendations on targeted repair vs. whole-home repiping, along with a clear cost comparison.

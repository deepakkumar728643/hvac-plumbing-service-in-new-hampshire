Bathroom Addition Plumbing in New Hampshire

Adding a bathroom to a New Hampshire home — whether finishing a basement, converting a bedroom, or building an addition — requires careful rough-in plumbing that meets NH plumbing code. This includes proper drain sizing and slope, venting for each fixture (or wet venting where code permits), supply line sizing, and coordination with structural elements.

We work closely with homeowners, designers, and general contractors to plan bathroom plumbing layouts that maximize functionality while minimizing cost. The key decisions — where to locate the toilet (which drives drain layout), how to vent the new bathroom, and whether to tap existing supply lines or run dedicated lines — should be made with a licensed plumber's input before walls are framed.

Fixture Upgrade & Replacement

Replacing an old toilet, vanity, or shower is one of the most impactful and affordable bathroom upgrades. We install all major fixture brands and can help you choose water-efficient models that reduce NH water bills without sacrificing performance. WaterSense-certified toilets use 1.28 gallons per flush vs. 3.5+ gpf for older models — a meaningful savings for families over the long run.

Tub-to-Shower Conversions

Tub-to-shower conversions are among the most requested bathroom plumbing projects in New Hampshire, particularly for aging-in-place renovations and for homeowners who simply prefer showers. The plumbing conversion involves modifying the drain location (if switching to a linear drain), capping or relocating the overflow, and plumbing the new shower valve and head. We also install curbless (zero-threshold) shower entries for accessibility.

What to Consider Before a Bathroom Remodel in NH

  • Permit requirements: Any structural, electrical, or significant plumbing work requires NH permits. We pull all required plumbing permits as part of every project.
  • Drain and vent access: The cost and complexity of bathroom plumbing depends heavily on where existing drain lines are and how easily they can be accessed. First-floor bathrooms above an accessible basement are the most straightforward. Upper-floor and slab-on-grade are more complex.
  • Water pressure: Adding fixtures increases demand on your supply system. If your home already has pressure issues, this is worth addressing before the remodel.
  • Insulating in exterior walls: Any pipes in exterior walls must be insulated to prevent freezing in NH winters. We flag and address this proactively on every project.

Common HVAC Problems in New Hampshire Homes

Furnace Won't Start

The most common causes of a furnace that won't start are a failed hot surface ignitor (these have a limited lifespan of 3–7 years), a faulty flame sensor that shuts the system down as a safety measure, a clogged or cracked inducer, or a tripped pressure switch. Most of these are same-day repairs that restore heat quickly. We carry common ignitors and sensors on every service vehicle.

Furnace Runs But Doesn't Heat

If your furnace is running but blowing cool air, the burners may be failing to light (ignitor or gas valve issue), the heat exchanger may have a crack triggering the high-limit switch, or the thermostat may be misconfigured. A cracked heat exchanger is a serious safety issue — it allows combustion gases including carbon monoxide to enter the airstream — and requires immediate professional assessment.

AC Not Cooling

Low refrigerant (from a leak), a dirty evaporator or condenser coil, failed capacitor, or a failing compressor are the most common causes of an AC system that runs but doesn't cool. We diagnose with manifold gauges, amp draw testing, and visual inspection to find the root cause before recommending any repair.

Heat Pump Running Constantly

In very cold weather, a cold-climate heat pump running more frequently is normal — it's working harder as outdoor temperature drops. But a system running all day and night without reaching setpoint may have a refrigerant issue, a defrost control problem, or be undersized for the load. We distinguish between normal operation and a genuine fault.

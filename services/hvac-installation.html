HVAC Installation Services in New Hampshire

Expert HVAC system installation for NH homes and businesses. Central AC, heat pumps, mini-splits, furnaces, and boilers — installed by licensed NH HVAC technicians with full manufacturer warranties.

Installing a new HVAC system is one of the most significant investments a New Hampshire homeowner can make — and one of the most impactful for year-round comfort and energy costs. New Hampshire's climate demands a lot from heating and cooling systems: brutally cold winters requiring reliable heat at -20°F, and humid summers that push cooling systems hard.

Plumbing HVAC Services New Hampshire installs the full range of residential and commercial HVAC systems, sized and configured for NH's specific climate requirements. Our licensed HVAC technicians are certified by EPA Section 608 and trained on the latest high-efficiency equipment.

HVAC Systems We Install in New Hampshire

Central Air Conditioning Systems

Central air conditioning systems use a network of ducts to distribute cooled air throughout the home. They're the most cost-effective cooling solution for homes that already have ductwork from an existing forced-air heating system. We install single-stage, two-stage, and variable-speed compressor systems — with variable-speed models offering the best combination of comfort, efficiency, and humidity control for NH summers.

Heat Pumps (Air-Source)

Air-source heat pumps are the fastest-growing HVAC category in New Hampshire, and for good reason. Modern cold-climate heat pumps (designed specifically for northern climates) operate efficiently down to -15°F or lower — making them genuinely viable as a primary heat source even in NH's harshest winters. Combined with their cooling function, they deliver year-round comfort from a single system.

New Hampshire offers excellent heat pump incentives through NHSaves, Eversource, Liberty Utilities, and the federal Inflation Reduction Act (up to $2,000 tax credit plus $300–$500 rebates from utilities). We help you navigate every available incentive program at the time of purchase.

Ductless Mini-Split Systems

Ductless mini-splits are ideal for homes without existing ductwork, room additions, finished basements, older homes, and zone-controlled comfort. Each indoor air handler operates independently, allowing different temperatures in different rooms. We install single-zone and multi-zone mini-split systems from Mitsubishi, Daikin, Fujitsu, LG, and other leading brands.

Furnaces (Gas & Propane)

Natural gas and propane furnaces remain the primary heating system in most New Hampshire homes. Modern high-efficiency condensing furnaces achieve 96–98% AFUE ratings, delivering significant fuel savings compared to older 80% AFUE units. We install furnaces from Carrier, Lennox, Trane, Bryant, and Rheem, and can match any budget and performance requirement.

Boilers & Hydronic Heating

Many older New Hampshire homes — and many discerning new homeowners — prefer the superior comfort of hydronic (hot water) heating. Boiler-based systems heat more evenly, maintain better humidity levels, and are exceptionally durable. We install and service oil, gas, and propane boilers, as well as related radiators, baseboard heaters, and in-floor radiant systems.

HVAC Installation Process

Choosing the Right HVAC System for Your NH Home

The right system depends on several factors specific to your home and situation:

  • Existing infrastructure: Does your home have ductwork? If so, ducted systems are typically more cost-effective. If not, ductless mini-splits or a mini-split + furnace hybrid may be ideal.
  • Fuel availability: Natural gas is available in most NH cities and larger towns. Propane is common in rural areas. Electric heat pumps have become economically competitive with all fuel types.
  • Home size and layout: Proper equipment sizing (Manual J load calculation) is critical. Oversized systems short-cycle and fail prematurely; undersized systems can't maintain comfort on the coldest NH days.
  • Budget vs. operating cost: Higher-efficiency equipment costs more upfront but delivers lower monthly energy bills. We'll provide a detailed payback analysis for any system we recommend.

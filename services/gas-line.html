Gas Line Installation in New Hampshire

New gas line installation is required whenever you're adding a gas appliance, converting from oil or electric to gas, extending service to an outdoor kitchen or fire pit, or connecting a whole-house generator. All gas line installation in New Hampshire requires permits and inspections by a state-licensed gas fitter — work we handle from permit application through final inspection.

We install both black iron pipe (the traditional material, still used for interior gas lines) and CSST (corrugated stainless steel tubing), which is approved for interior use in New Hampshire when properly bonded. Outdoor and underground runs use coated steel or PE gas pipe approved for burial.

Gas Leak Detection & Repair

Gas leaks are a serious safety emergency. If you suspect a gas leak — detected by smell (the odorant added to natural gas smells like rotten eggs), hearing a hissing sound near a gas line, or seeing dead vegetation above a buried gas line — leave the building immediately and call 911. Once the scene is declared safe, our licensed gas fitters can locate the leak precisely using electronic gas leak detectors and pressure testing, then repair or replace the affected pipe or fitting.

Propane Gas Line Services in NH

Many rural New Hampshire homes and businesses rely on propane because natural gas service is not available in their area. Propane lines serve the same appliances as natural gas but at different pressures and require different fittings and regulators. We're fully equipped for propane gas line work and coordinate with your propane supplier as needed.

Gas Safety in New Hampshire Homes

Beyond emergency response, gas safety is a matter of routine vigilance. We recommend:

  • Install and test CO detectors on every floor and near sleeping areas — replace every 5–7 years
  • Have gas appliances and lines inspected every 5 years by a licensed gas fitter
  • Know the location of every gas shutoff in your home
  • Never use gas ovens or ranges for heating
  • Keep combustibles away from gas appliances
  • Respond immediately to any unusual smell — don't ignore it

Signs You Need Professional Drain Cleaning in NH

Many homeowners try DIY drain cleaners first — but store-bought chemical products typically provide only temporary relief and can actually damage pipes over time. Professional drain cleaning is the right call when you notice:

  • 🔴 Multiple slow or clogged drains simultaneously — a sign of a main line blockage, not an isolated clog
  • 🔴 Gurgling sounds from drains or toilets when water runs elsewhere
  • 🔴 Sewage odors coming from drains, especially in the basement
  • 🔴 Water backing up into sinks, tubs, or the basement floor drain
  • 🔴 Recurring clogs in the same drain despite repeated attempts to clear it
  • 🔴 Slow drains that never seem to fully clear after using commercial products

Our Drain Cleaning Methods

Professional Drain Snaking (Mechanical Augering)

For most household clogs — hair, soap buildup, food waste, minor grease accumulation — a professional drain snake (also called a plumber's auger or rooter) is the fastest and most effective solution. Unlike the small hand-cranked snakes available at hardware stores, our electric-powered professional augers reach deep into the drain system and physically break up or retrieve the obstruction. We carry cable machines from 25-foot hand snakes to 100-foot main line augers.

Hydro-Jetting

Hydro-jetting uses highly pressurized water (typically 3,000–4,000 PSI) to scour pipe walls clean from the inside. It's the most thorough drain cleaning method available and the preferred solution for:

  • Heavy grease buildup in kitchen drain lines
  • Mineral scale accumulation (common with NH hard water)
  • Recurring clogs that keep coming back after snaking
  • Pre-inspection cleaning before lining or pipe repair
  • Commercial kitchens and high-use commercial drains

Hydro-jetting restores pipes to near-original flow capacity and is safe for most pipe materials including PVC, ABS, cast iron, and clay tile.

Video Sewer Camera Inspection

When a clog is stubborn, recurring, or suspected to be caused by something more serious (root intrusion, pipe collapse, offset joints), we deploy a high-definition sewer camera. This lets us see exactly what's happening inside the pipe — identifying not just the blockage but any underlying structural issues. We provide recorded footage and a written assessment, which can be valuable documentation if insurance or municipal claims are involved.

Tree Root Intrusion in NH Sewer Lines

One of the most common causes of severe main sewer line clogs in New Hampshire — especially in older neighborhoods with mature trees — is root intrusion. Tree roots follow moisture and nutrients, making sewer lines an ideal growth path. Once roots enter through a joint or crack, they expand over months and years, eventually causing complete blockages or pipe collapse.

We clear root obstructions using a combination of mechanical root cutting and hydro-jetting, then assess whether pipe repair or replacement is needed. Caught early, root intrusion can be managed before it becomes a major excavation project.

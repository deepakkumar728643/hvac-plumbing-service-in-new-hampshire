Bathroom Remodeling

Bathroom Remodel Plumbing Cost in New Hampshire (2024 Guide)

By Plumbing HVAC Services NH | June 15, 2024 | 7 min read

A bathroom renovation is one of the most popular home improvement projects in New Hampshire — and plumbing is typically the most complex and costly component. Whether you're doing a simple fixture refresh or a full gut-and-rebuild, understanding realistic plumbing costs in NH will help you budget accurately and avoid surprises. Here's a comprehensive breakdown.

Average Bathroom Plumbing Costs in New Hampshire

NH plumbing labor rates are influenced by local licensing requirements, supply costs, and regional demand. Here are typical ranges for 2024:

  • Basic fixture replacement (toilet, sink, faucets — same location): $800–$2,000
  • Standard bathroom remodel (move some fixtures, new tub/shower): $3,500–$8,000 plumbing only
  • Full bathroom gut and rebuild (new everything, possible relocation): $6,000–$15,000+ plumbing only
  • Adding a new half bath or full bath to existing NH home: $5,000–$12,000 plumbing only

These are plumbing-only costs — they don't include tile, fixtures, cabinetry, flooring, or general contractor fees. Total bathroom renovation costs in NH typically run $15,000–$40,000+ for a full mid-range remodel.

What Affects Plumbing Costs in Your NH Bathroom?

Moving Fixtures vs Replacing in Place

The single biggest cost driver is whether you're moving plumbing. Replacing a toilet in the same footprint with a new toilet costs $200–$500 in labor. Moving that toilet 3 feet involves cutting into the subfloor, rerouting drain lines, and potentially rerouting venting — a $1,500–$3,000 job. Every fixture move adds significant cost.

Existing Pipe Condition

New Hampshire has a substantial stock of homes with galvanized steel supply pipes (common in homes built before 1970) or cast iron drain pipes. These often need replacement during a remodel — not because you planned to, but because once opened up, their poor condition is apparent. Budget for the possibility, especially in older NH homes.

Shower or Tub Type

A simple tub-to-shower conversion with a prefab enclosure is the most affordable option ($800–$1,500 plumbing). A custom tile shower with a bench, multiple showerheads, and a linear drain requires significantly more plumbing rough-in work ($2,500–$5,000+ for plumbing alone). Steam showers add a steam generator and require dedicated plumbing and electrical.

NH Permit Requirements

Most NH municipalities require plumbing permits for bathroom renovations, especially when moving fixtures or adding new ones. Permit costs typically run $100–$400 in NH, and all permitted work requires inspections. A licensed NH plumber handles all permit applications and inspections as part of the job — be wary of contractors who suggest skipping permits.

Plumbing Fixtures: What They Cost in NH (2024)

  • Toilet (installed): $400–$1,200 depending on model
  • Bathroom sink and faucet (installed): $350–$900
  • Bathtub (installed, alcove): $700–$2,500
  • Walk-in shower with enclosure (installed): $2,000–$5,000
  • Shower valve and trim (installed): $400–$1,200

How to Save on Bathroom Plumbing in NH

Plan ahead and make all design decisions before work begins — change orders during active renovation are expensive. Keep fixtures in their existing locations whenever possible. Avoid the most budget-stretching items: multiple showerheads, in-floor radiant heat under tile, and steam systems. And always get 2–3 written estimates from licensed NH plumbers.

Most importantly: hire licensed plumbers. Unlicensed work that doesn't pass inspection can result in having to redo everything — at your expense.
